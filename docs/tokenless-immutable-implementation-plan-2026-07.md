# Tokenless, Keyless RateLoop — Implementation Plan (July 2026)

**Status:** v2, 2026-07-11. Companion to [web3-exit-stablecoin-pivot-analysis-2026-07.md](web3-exit-stablecoin-pivot-analysis-2026-07.md), which holds the strategic case. v1 answered: (1) can Hawig UG hold no upgrade keys, (2) are USDC vote bonds a gambling-law risk, (3) instant resolution vs commit-reveal. v2 adds two follow-ups and changes one recommendation: (4) is the vote bond needed at all — **no, remove it entirely** (v1's "returnable deposit" design is now the fallback, not the plan), and (5) do correlation epoch snapshots and the challenge-window oracle make sense when a single entity (Hawig Ventures UG) is the only proposer, challenger, and arbiter — **no, replace with transparency-log publication**.

---

## TL;DR

1. **Keyless is achievable and worth it, but it is currently far from true:** today every core contract is a `TransparentUpgradeableProxy` with a governance timelock as upgrade admin, plus pause, sweep, and ~30 governance setters. The plan converts everything to direct, constructor-configured, non-upgradeable deployments with no pause/sweep/setters (the Liquity model: bug response = disclose → let users exit → redeploy). Legally, keylessness clears the sharpest MiCA "intermediary" indicators (admin keys, governance concentration) but is necessary, not sufficient — the sole frontend and the fee keep Hawig UG identifiable. Honest positioning: *"non-custodial and immutable — we cannot touch user funds, verify on-chain,"* not "decentralized."
2. **Remove the rater bond entirely — don't redesign it.** v1 already had to gut the bond's mechanism role (German gambling law forbids the forfeit-to-winners structure; no de-minimis exception exists). What remained was a returnable deposit whose only job was spam pricing — and stake-free crowd judgment is the *norm*, not the exception: Community Notes, Code4rena, Sherlock, and Metaculus all run free-entry with layered non-monetary defenses. Zero rater payment is also the legally airtight position: with no Entgelt, § 3 GlüStV cannot apply at all (gas paid to third-party validators is doctrinally like postage — not consideration; sponsored gas removes even that). Bonus: the voting engine then holds **no rater funds whatsoever** — only funder bounties sit in escrow — which shrinks the MiCA custody and EMT/PSD2 surface again and deletes the entire stake/forfeit/permit machinery from the contracts.
3. **What replaces the bond is layered gating, not nothing** (§A.4): identity tiers where forgery cost exceeds capped per-identity extraction (World ID; Gitcoin's cost-of-forgery math), probationary reputation before payout-eligible voting (the Community Notes/Sherlock pattern — reputation, not money, is the slashable resource), payout functions that zero uninformative votes, verified-only eligibility plus minimum round sizes for high-value rounds (peer-prediction scoring breaks if sybils reach round majority — this parameter matters more than any bond ever did), World-ID-gated gas sponsorship (World Chain's exact playbook), and — held in reserve — x402 per-submission pricing for agent lanes, with the explicit caveat that an operator-collected entry fee reopens the Entgelt analysis.
4. **The correlation-oracle challenge game is security theater in single-operator form — delete it, keep the math as a transparency log.** By the industry's own standard (L2Beat stages; the verifier's-dilemma literature; UMA's post-Polymarket-attack reform), a challenge window without an independent, motivated watcher set secures nothing: Hawig would be proposer, only-plausible challenger, and arbiter of itself, with a 5 USDC bond deterring nobody. Gitcoin — the origin of the COCM cluster-matching idea — runs it exactly the other way: computed off-chain by one team, code public, results posted for community review, then applied. Nobody anywhere built an on-chain-challengeable COCM besides this repo. Replacement (§A.5): settle directly from revealed votes; publish per-round inputs, algorithm commit, and a recompute script (Community Notes pattern); keep correlation analytics off-chain feeding *future-round eligibility* (never retroactive payout reversal); pre-commit to an app-layer remediation policy — the thing Polymarket's refusal made a reputational disaster, and which a company operator can offer credibly.
5. **Single-entity keeper is fine — because every keeper-called function is permissionless.** The keeper becomes a liveness convenience, not a trust point: anyone can run reveal/settle against the public drand beacon. Mitigations are documentation (a run-your-own-keeper script), optionally a Gelato/Chainlink Automation backup registration (trivial cost on Base), and that's it. No authority treats submitting transactions to permissionless functions as a licensable activity. What made the keeper a genuine trust point — snapshot proposing and the arbiter role — is deleted with the oracle.
6. **Keep on-chain commit-reveal; payouts become near-instant anyway.** Commit-reveal is mechanism-essential (public on-chain votes ⇒ herding without sealing), not regulatory. The latency that matters today comes from the permissionless-trust machinery this plan deletes (challenge/veto windows, 1-hour snapshot timeout, fee reviews, unbonding). Target end-to-end: **question duration + ~1–2 minutes** — and with bonds gone there is no forfeiture logic in the reveal path at all, so it gets simpler still.

## Part A — The questions, answered from research

### A.1 Keyless immutable design: what it buys, what it doesn't

**Current admin surface (must all go):** every stateful contract behind a proxy with timelock upgrade admin (`ContentRegistry`, `RoundVotingEngine`, `ProtocolConfig`, `RoundRewardDistributor`, `FrontendRegistry`, `ProfileRegistry`, all three escrows, `FeedbackRegistry`, `RaterRegistry`); `ProtocolConfig` with 19 governance setters; pausability on `ContentRegistry`, `RoundVotingEngine`, escrows; sweeps/rescues (`recoverSurplusLrep`, `sweepStrandedLrepToTreasury`, `recoverNonAssetToken`, `rescueToken`); a live `ARBITER_ROLE` on `ClusterPayoutOracle` that can reject payout snapshots; and the Governor/timelock stack itself ([Deploy.s.sol](../packages/foundry/script/Deploy.s.sol)).

**Legal value of removing it (MFSA DeFi paper, June 2026; Commission MiCA-review indicators):** regulators score decentralization on admin keys/upgradeability, governance concentration, custody, open source, identifiable marketing entity, and fee control. Keyless immutability decisively clears the first two; an *immutable hard-coded* fee is weaker evidence of control than a settable fee (untested but directionally right). What keylessness cannot clear: Hawig UG runs the sole frontend, markets the product, and earns the fee — under substance-over-form it stays the identifiable responsible person. Two consequences:

- Don't oversell "decentralized"; the honest claim is *"non-custodial and immutable: we cannot touch, freeze, or redirect user funds — verify on-chain."* That claim is true, checkable, and is exactly what the custody-by-control analysis needs.
- Mitigate the sole-frontend point cheaply: publish verified contract addresses as the canonical interface, pin a frontend build to IPFS, document direct-contract usage, and make any future migration opt-in with the old deployment left running (Liquity precedent).

**The cost of immutability is the bug story.** Liquity V2's Feb 2025 bug is the playbook: no fix path existed; they disclosed, users withdrew (~$30M outflows, zero losses), re-audited, redeployed three months later. The design rule that made that survivable: **every state must have a user exit path** — bounty funders must always be able to withdraw from a wedged round. Make it an explicit invariant with Foundry tests; add minimal contract surface (this plan deletes most of it), a pre-launch audit competition, and an Immunefi-style bounty. Nexus Mutual cover exists but is user-purchased — mention in docs, don't rely on it.

**One deliberate exception to "no keys," to decide explicitly:** World ID router/verifier config in `RaterRegistry` (World has shipped v3→v4 and will ship again). Options: (a) freeze at deploy (the freeze flags exist: `worldIdV4VerifierConfigFrozen`, `worldIdV4PresenceConfigFrozen`) and redeploy the registry on World upgrades; (b) one narrowly-scoped, publicly documented role that can only rotate World ID verifier config and touches no funds. This plan assumes (a). Note the eligibility/reputation attestations in §A.4 run through the existing backend-issuer pattern — an operator-controlled *eligibility* gate, which is fine: it gates who can earn, never where escrowed money goes.

### A.2 Vote bonds and gambling law — why the answer is now "remove them"

**Why the risk was real (unchanged from v1):** § 3 GlüStV deems chance-dependence present when winning turns on uncertain future events ("predict % of others voting up" is facially a bet on third-party behavior); the average-player standard made even poker gambling (BVerwG 8 C 26.12); OVG NRW (13 B 1047/22, 2024) rejected any de-minimis stake threshold; the GGL declared prediction markets unlicensable "Gesellschaftswetten" and blacklisted Polymarket (Sept 2025); crypto-denominated gambling is per-se unlicensable (§ 4 GlüStV) — so classification would be binary and existential, with § 284/285 StGB criminal exposure. The fatal design element was `RewardMath.splitPool` routing 96% of forfeited stakes to well-scoring raters: losers' stakes funding winners' prizes is precisely the poker-tournament structure.

**v1's fix was a returnable deposit (Kaution). v2 goes further: no rater payment at all.** The deciding facts:

- **The deposit's only surviving job was spam pricing** — v1 had already removed score-based forfeiture, so the bond neither carried RBTS incentives nor secured reveal (the keeper reveals everyone via drand tlock; raters take no reveal action).
- **Zero payment is doctrinally airtight, not just defensible.** With no Entgelt, § 3 GlüStV cannot apply — a free-entry, merit-judged payout scheme is a Gewinnspiel/Auslobung (§§ 657, 661 BGB), no gambling license needed. The "hidden stake" (versteckter Einsatz) doctrine doesn't bite: ordinary third-party transmission costs are not consideration (the postage/telephone-cost line), and sub-cent Base gas paid to validators — not to Hawig, not into the bounty — is the same category; World-ID-gated gas sponsorship removes even that residue.
- **UX and custody wins:** raters never approve, deposit, or risk anything; the engine holds no rater funds, so the MiCA custody-by-control and EMT/PSD2 "on behalf of clients" questions apply only to the funder-side escrows; and the entire stake/permit/forfeit/sweep machinery disappears from `RoundVotingEngine`.

**Two watch-outs the research added:**

1. **Operator-collected participation fees would reopen everything.** If agent lanes later charge a per-submission x402 fee *to Hawig*, that is a payment demanded for a chance at bounty share — the Entgelt analysis restarts for those rounds (fallback arguments: skill predominance, fee-covers-costs). Launch with no submission fees (agents pay their own gas); if spam economics later force per-submission pricing, do it with counsel and keep it structurally separate from free human rounds.
2. **Labor law (BAG 9 AZR 102/20, the crowdworker ruling):** a platform whose gamified incentive ladder (levels, streaks, time pressure) steers users into continuous, instruction-bound micro-work can turn raters into employees regardless of contract labels. Keep rating genuinely voluntary — no obligation to accept tasks, no streak mechanics that induce continuous performance. Occasional per-round payouts to freely-choosing raters are far from the fact pattern; a "power-rater career ladder" is where risk would accumulate. Rater payouts are the rater's taxable income either way — say so in the terms.

With bonds gone, the formal gambling-law opinion downgrades from launch gate to **confirmatory check** — it becomes a gate again only if any operator-collected participation fee is ever introduced.

### A.3 Latency: what "almost instant" actually requires

Current end-to-end chain and verdict on each element:

| Stage | Today | Essential? | Plan |
|---|---|---|---|
| Blind vote window | 20 min default (20 s floor – 60 d cap) | **Yes — product, not crypto.** Time for humans to answer; also the sealing period | Keep as per-question knob; agents wanting fast answers set short windows and higher bounties |
| tlock commit-reveal (drand) | Ciphertexts decryptable seconds after window ends; keeper reveals | **Yes — mechanism.** On-chain votes are public; without sealing, raters herd and peer-prediction scoring collapses. Bonus: even the operator can't peek (matters for confidential asks and agent trust) | Keep unchanged |
| Reveal grace | 60 min | Partly. Raters don't reveal manually — the keeper decrypts everyone; grace covers keeper redundancy. With no bonds there is no forfeiture — an unrevealable ciphertext simply earns nothing | Shrink to minutes (bounded keeper-retry budget) |
| RBTS settlement-pending on correlation oracle | Parks every non-tied round; 1 h timeout | **No — permissionless-trust artifact** | Delete with the oracle (§A.5) |
| Oracle challenge + veto windows | 15 min + 15 min (up to days) | No — same | Delete |
| Frontend fee review / unbonding | 1 h / 14 d | No — slashing-review machinery for bonded third-party frontends | Delete with the LREP bond model |
| Reward claim graces | 7 d claim / 30 d stale / 1 h feedback-award | Claim-safety, tunable | Keep; they backstop cleanup, not payout availability |

**Result: after the blind window closes, reveal → settle → claimable payout in ~1–2 minutes, in a design more trustless than today's (no arbiter role, no operator-challengeable snapshots).** The only way to go faster is a shorter blind window — a per-question choice the funder controls. Dropping commit-reveal for a trusted instant tally would (a) make vote privacy depend on the operator (the confidential-pretesting use case depends on nobody, including us, seeing early votes), (b) weaken the self-execution posture the custody analysis rests on, (c) remove the "verifiably sealed round" property that differentiates the product — all to save under two minutes. Short-cycle instant money games also pattern-match toward gambling in German practice (the GGL's DFS action) — a mild extra reason not to chase instant resolution.

### A.4 Stake-free sybil and spam defense (replaces the bond)

Free entry + positive expected payout + near-zero marginal cost means the *only* defense is making an uninformative vote's expected reward ≈ 0 and bounding per-identity extraction. Every serious stake-free platform converged on the same layers — adopt them all:

1. **Identity tiers with cost-of-forgery math (the load-bearing layer).** Gitcoin's frame: an identity tier is sound when forging it costs more than the capped extraction it unlocks (their fraud tax fell 6.6% → ~0.6% of pool after Passport gating). For RateLoop: World ID Orb > passport credential > device > unverified, with per-identity round caps and daily rate limits per tier. Creator-chosen verified-only eligibility (already built) is *the* control for high-value rounds.
2. **Probationary reputation before payout-eligible voting.** Community Notes admits raters (aged account + carrier-verified unique phone) into rating-only probation and unlocks writing via Rating Impact — outcome-aligned, negative-capable reputation. Sherlock prices escalations in earned "Signal," not money. RateLoop equivalent: new unverified wallets vote advisory-only (the `AdvisoryVoteRecorder` tier, which already exists, minus its launch-credit minting); payout-eligible voting is unlocked by track record and/or World ID, issued as an on-chain attestation via the existing backend-issuer pattern. Reputation — not money — becomes the slashable resource, and it's worthless to sell.
3. **Payout functions that zero uninformative work.** Sherlock pays zero for low-quality findings; MTurk-style gold questions catch random clickers. RBTS score-weighting already does this — keep a hard floor under which a vote earns nothing (not "little").
4. **Round-size and majority awareness — the parameter that matters most.** Peer-prediction scoring is computed relative to the voter population: if sybils are the round majority, they *define* the peer distribution and BTS rewards them (documented collusion equilibria in the peer-prediction literature). Consequences: minimum revealed-voter floors scale with bounty size; small high-value rounds must be verified-only; per-round voter caps cut both ways (they also make majority capture cheaper) — cap per-identity, not per-round, where possible.
5. **Sponsorship gating (World Chain's exact playbook).** Sponsor gas only for World-ID-verified raters, tiered daily budgets per verification level, CDP paymaster with contract allowlist + per-address/global spend caps behind a backend proxy. Unverified and agent traffic pays its own sub-cent gas.
6. **Agent lanes: price, don't bond (held in reserve).** For agent-welcome rounds the right lever is unit economics, not identity: a small non-refundable x402 submission fee ≥ the expected extraction of an uninformative vote. Not at launch (see A.2 watch-out 1 — Entgelt), but the design slot stays open.
7. **Post-hoc enforcement without payout reversal.** LayerZero-style sybil hunts and quest-platform history say farming *will* be attempted. Detection (correlation analytics, §A.5) feeds eligibility bans and attestation revocation for *future* rounds — settled payouts are never clawed back (no admin path exists anyway).

### A.5 Correlation snapshots and the keeper: single-entity reality check

**The challenge-window oracle is theater with one operator — by the sector's own standard.** L2Beat's stage framework treats whitelisted proposer/challenger sets as disqualifying ("Stage 0 training wheels"); the verifier's-dilemma literature is explicit that optimistic security is void without at least one honest, diligent, *independent* watcher; and the March 2025 UMA/Polymarket incident ($7M market falsely resolved by a whale across the challenge window; no refunds) showed that the parties who actually engage dispute machinery are financially motivated adversaries, not guardians. UMA's own fix (UMIP-189, Aug 2025) was to *whitelist proposers* and keep disputes open — i.e., the flagship optimistic oracle concluded trusted proposers are fine when the **disputer** side is real. RateLoop's single-operator setting is the inverse: trusted proposer exists, disputer set is empty by construction, and "governance arbitrates" reduces to Hawig arbitrating Hawig. A 5 USDC challenge bond deters nobody. The machinery costs contract surface (1,462-line oracle + libs), keeper complexity (the largest share of `keeper.ts`), and an hour+ of settlement latency, and buys a decentralization *signal* that MiCA's substance-over-form analysis sees straight through anyway.

**The precedent for RateLoop's shape is Gitcoin — and it points off-chain.** COCM cluster-matching (the algorithm family these snapshots implement) is run by Gitcoin exactly as: computed post-round off-chain by one team, open-source code, results posted for a ~5-day community review, then applied. No bonds, no on-chain challenge game — and no one else has ever built an on-chain-challengeable COCM. Community Notes generalizes the pattern: open-source the scoring algorithm, publish the data, let anyone recompute and publicly flag (Vitalik's praise of it is literally "download the data, run the algorithm, verify the output"). Certificate-transparency logs formalize it: tamper-evident publication whose value is monitorability, at near-zero operating cost.

**Recommendation — delete the oracle, keep the math, publish the evidence:**

1. `RoundVotingEngine.settleRound` settles **directly from revealed votes** (equal base weights; RBTS scoring unchanged) — no `SettlementPending`, no snapshot consumer paths, no arbiter.
2. Keep the deterministic correlation-artifact computation as an **off-chain analytics job** (the `correlation-artifact-builder` code survives as a library): its output feeds §A.4's eligibility bans and attestation revocations for future rounds — never retroactive payout changes.
3. **Transparency log:** per round, publish inputs (revealed votes), the algorithm version (commit hash), outputs, and a one-command recompute script; anchor a periodic hash on-chain if desired (cheap, optional). This replaces the challenge window with something anyone can actually use.
4. **Pre-committed remediation policy:** because a company operator — unlike a DAO — can credibly offer it: if manipulation of a settled round is demonstrated (recompute mismatch or eligibility fraud), Hawig refunds the funder's fee/bounty at the app layer per published policy. Polymarket's refusal to do this after March 2025 was its biggest reputational hit; this is cheap insurance and honest about who the operator is.
5. **Later, if trustlessness is ever demanded:** the 2026 upgrade path is validity proofs (zk-coprocessors proving the off-chain computation), or an opt-in new contract version with on-chain weighting — never a revived challenge game, and never an admin key.

**The keeper, post-oracle:** what remains (tlock reveal, settle, cleanup) is all permissionless-callable, so a single Hawig-run keeper is a liveness convenience, not a trust point — the accepted norm. Mitigations: publish a run-your-own-keeper script (anyone can decrypt via the public drand beacon and call reveal/settle), optionally register a Gelato or Chainlink Automation backup upkeep (trivial cost on Base), alert on settlement backlog as today. Base's private mempool and the calls' lack of extractable ordering value make MEV a non-issue; L1 force-inclusion covers sequencer censorship. No licensing angle to submitting transactions to public functions was found (flagged as absence-of-evidence). The parts of keeper work that *were* genuine trust points — snapshot proposal, challenge monitoring, the arbiter — are exactly the parts being deleted.

**Fee:** hard-coded protocol fee (percentage bps for bounties + flat-cent x402 path), immutable `PROTOCOL_FEE_RECIPIENT = Hawig UG address`, charged at escrow funding/payout sites — **funder-side only**; raters pay nothing (§A.2). Fee changes = redeploy = visible to everyone.

**Trust posture, published as a short TRUST.md:** immutable contracts (addresses + verification links), no admin keys, operator cannot touch funds, raters never deposit funds, votes sealed via drand tlock, settlement recomputable from public reveals (link the recompute script), remediation policy (§A.5.4), bug policy = disclose → exit → redeploy, run-your-own-keeper instructions.

## Part C — Phased plan

**Phase 0 — Gates (before any code):**
1. EMT/PSD2 "on behalf of clients" opinion on the **funder-side** escrow/payout flow (the rater side no longer exists). *Blocking for mainnet.*
2. Confirmatory German gambling-law check on the zero-Entgelt design (A.2) — downgraded from v1's launch gate; becomes a gate again only if operator-collected participation fees are ever added. Include the `ConfidentialityEscrow` bond question (Part B note).
3. Mechanism review: RBTS incentive properties with no stakes and no negative-spread forfeiture — reward-side score weighting + zero-floor + §A.4 gating must carry truthful reporting; validate minimum-voter floors vs bounty size (§A.4.4).
4. Decisions: World ID config frozen-at-deploy vs narrow rotation role (A.1); initial eligibility-tier matrix (which verification level unlocks which bounty sizes).

**Phase 1 — Mechanism spec (small):** write the new round lifecycle — free commit (eligibility-gated per tier), tlock reveal, direct RBTS settlement with equal base weights and zero-floor, bounty split by score, advisory probation tier, no rater funds anywhere. This spec anchors everything below.

**Phase 2 — Contract surgery (the big one; order matters):**
1. Delete `LoopReputation`, governance stack, `LaunchDistributionPool`, `ClusterPayoutOracle` + libs, and their deploy-script sections ([Deploy.s.sol:302–445, 666–732](../packages/foundry/script/Deploy.s.sol)).
2. `RoundVotingEngine`: remove stake entirely (`MIN_STAKE`/`MAX_STAKE`, `commitVoteWithPermit`, `accountedLrepBalance`, forfeit pools, `recoverSurplusLrep`, `flushPendingTreasuryForfeit`); commit becomes eligibility-checked and fund-free; delete oracle dependency (`settleRound` settles directly; RBTS module scores from revealed data); delete pause/role machinery; rewrite `RewardMath` (bounty-share weighting + zero-floor; `splitPool` forfeit logic deleted — nothing to forfeit).
3. Escrows: delete LREP branches; immutable fee bps + recipient; delete pause + rescue functions; verify funder exit-path invariants.
4. `ContentRegistry`: submission/revival USDC; delete setters, pause, treasury mutability.
5. `FrontendRegistry` → attribution-only (or delete; fold fee-share into ask structs + `RoundRewardDistributor`).
6. `RaterRegistry`: freeze World ID config at deploy; add track-record attestation kind via the backend-issuer pattern.
7. De-proxy everything: `initialize()` → constructors with immutables, deploy direct, no ProxyAdmin. `ProtocolConfig` becomes constants.
8. Deploy script: single-shot deploy, zero retained roles; assert-no-admin post-deploy verification script.
9. Foundry tests: prune LREP/governance/oracle/stake suites (a large share of the ~106k test LOC); add invariants for "no admin path to funds," "engine holds no rater funds," and "funder exit always available."

**Phase 3 — Services (medium):** keeper: delete snapshot/challenge/arbiter jobs and metrics, keep reveal/settle/cleanup; add the transparency-log publisher + off-chain correlation analytics job (§A.5); ponder: drop deleted-contract handlers and LREP fields; regenerate `@rateloop/contracts`.

**Phase 4 — App + SDK (medium):** nextjs: remove governance/tokenomics/claim/launch pages, LREP wallet UX, stake selection UI (`StakeSelector` goes away — voting is deposit-free), `votingTypes.ts` LREP fields; add eligibility-tier UX and World-ID-gated sponsorship; update `/docs` to the tokenless story + TRUST.md; sdk/agents: USDC-only types, unchanged agent flow; e2e suites updated.

**Phase 5 — Assurance + launch:** internal review → audit competition on the (much smaller) immutable core → Immunefi bounty → Base Sepolia soak with agents on the x402 lane → mainnet single-shot deploy → publish TRUST.md, transparency log, recompute script, keeper runbook.

Rough overall effort: Phase 2 dominates and is even more deletion-heavy than v1 (stake machinery now goes too). End state: a fraction of today's ~32k LOC contract surface, a keeper reduced to its settlement core plus a transparency publisher, and a rater UX with zero deposits, zero approvals, and optional zero gas.

## Part D — Risks and open decisions

1. **Sybil majorities in small rounds are now the top mechanism risk** (was: bond design). Peer-prediction scoring rewards a coordinated majority; the defenses are eligibility tiers, minimum revealed-voter floors scaled to bounty size, and verified-only high-value rounds (§A.4.4). Treat the tier/floor matrix as a first-class launch parameter and monitor with the correlation analytics from day one.
2. **Reputation-gaming replaces stake-gaming:** build-honest-then-spend attacks on probationary reputation are documented against Community Notes-style systems. Mitigations: negative-capable reputation, revocable attestations, correlation analytics feeding revocation. Accept as residual.
3. **EMT/PSD2 opinion could still come back adverse on the funder-side escrows** — unchanged from v1, now the only remaining fund-flow question.
4. **Immutability + bug = redeploy.** Accepted consciously; exit-path invariant, small surface, audit competition. Never ship a "temporary" pause.
5. **World ID config freezing** means a registry redeploy when World ships v5 — accepted (registry holds no funds). Fallback: the narrow rotation role, documented.
6. **Sole-frontend intermediary optics** remain regardless of keys — mitigated by contract-first docs, IPFS mirror, honest "non-custodial, not decentralized-governance" language.
7. **Transparency logs only bite if someone recomputes** (the Sigstore caveat). Make recomputation one command, invite it in docs, and consider a small standing bounty for demonstrated mismatches — a real challenger incentive, priced honestly, without the on-chain theater.
8. **Agent-lane pricing is deferred, not solved:** if inference-cost spam materializes in open rounds before x402 submission pricing is legally cleared, the interim lever is eligibility tiers and per-identity caps on unverified/agent traffic.
