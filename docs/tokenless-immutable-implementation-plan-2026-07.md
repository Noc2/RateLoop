# RateLoop Tokenless: Architecture & Implementation Plan (July 2026)

**Status:** Current design of record. Rationale in [web3-exit-stablecoin-pivot-analysis-2026-07.md](web3-exit-stablecoin-pivot-analysis-2026-07.md); legal/revenue reference in [legal-revenue-assessment-tokenless-design-2026-07.md](legal-revenue-assessment-tokenless-design-2026-07.md).

## Execution philosophy: build-first

The entire system is implemented immediately (AI-driven development) and tested live end-to-end. Legal grey areas are tracked in a parallel backlog and addressed over time — they do not gate building or testing; if something breaks legally or technically, the design changes or the project is abandoned. Consequences:

- **v0 deployments are disposable.** With ~zero users and test-scale money, iterate-by-redeploy is free. The keyless/immutable posture hardens into a real commitment only once third-party money flows at scale.
- **Only a short pre-real-money list blocks scaled launch** (each item cheap, and existential or contract-shaped): paid-panel framing (never "reviews"), Impressum + AGB skeleton, sanctions geoblocking + basic wallet screening, the DAC7 get-paid sheet in the eligibility flow, B2B-only funders, and the contract-schema defaults below.

## Core architecture decisions

1. **Chain: Base.** Identity is off-chain and commits are relayed, so nothing needs a specific chain; the demand side (x402 facilitator + agent ecosystem, CDP Paymaster, Coinbase Onramp, USDC liquidity, Base Account) is Base-native. World Chain was evaluated and declined — its advantages (World-App distribution, verified-human free gas, Mini Apps) serve an acquisition channel the product does not target; contracts are portable if that ever changes. Rater surface: **mobile-web PWA**; World App is only an optional verification handoff (deep-link out/back).
2. **Keyless, immutable contracts.** No proxies, no pause, no sweeps, no setters; constructor-configured; single-shot deploy with an assert-no-admin verification script. One documented exception: the credential issuer contract keeps a narrowly-scoped signer-rotation admin (it gates eligibility only, never funds). Bug response = disclose → let users exit → redeploy; every state must have a funder exit path (Foundry invariant). Honest positioning: *"non-custodial and immutable — no operator path to funds"* — not "decentralized."
3. **Stake-free voting.** Raters never deposit, approve, or risk anything (zero Entgelt ⇒ German gambling law cannot apply; the engine holds no rater funds, shrinking the MiCA/PSD2 surface to funder-side escrows). Quality/sybil control comes from gates, not money: identity tiers, per-identity caps enforced at voucher issuance, gold questions, probation for unverified wallets, minimum round sizes scaled to bounty.
4. **Voting stays on-chain** (commit-reveal with drand/tlock sealing). Self-submitted votes are the one input the operator cannot forge; an operator-posted settlement root over escrowed funds would be a de facto admin key and would worsen both the MiCA custody-by-control and PSD2 analyses. Costs are solved on Base: sub-cent sponsored commits, no popups (see 6).
5. **Pseudonymous vote keys.** Votes are cast from per-round one-time keys unlinkable on-chain to the payout wallet (a vote is an opinion = personal data; the EDPB blockchain guidelines discourage identifiable on-chain data). The commit voucher carries a per-(identity, round) nullifier, deduped on-chain — one-vote-per-identity is contract-enforced without cross-round linkage. The rater binds a blinded payout commitment into their own commit, so settlement and claims are self-contained from user inputs. The vote-key↔rater mapping is held off-chain, encrypted under per-rater keys; erasure = deletion after statutory retention periods. Residual linkability (claim-time reveal, amount correlation in small rounds, funding graphs for self-funded keys) is stated honestly; unlinkability requires the sponsored relay path.
6. **Commit authorization is decoupled from submission.** The contract verifies the voucher-bound vote key's signature over the commit payload and ignores `msg.sender`; commits are relayed (RateLoop's sponsored relayer, any relayer, or self-funded). No per-key account deployment; no wallet interaction to vote. The rater's wallet (Base Account passkey, self-custodial) matters only at payout. Sub Accounts / Spend Permissions are used in funder flows only. Never build in-house key management (licensable crypto custody in Germany); defer TEE-vendor embedded wallets until passkey drop-off data demands one.
7. **Identity off-chain, tiers funder-selectable.** All verification happens off-chain (World ID cloud API today; provider swappable), issued as expiring EIP-712 credential attestations by the issuer contract's backend signer. Tier semantics: selfie = "live human now" (no uniqueness — low caps), passport = per-document uniqueness (the non-biometric path, required to reach every earning tier), Orb = global uniqueness (price forgery at the ~$30 account black-market rate), presence recheck stacks on any base credential against account rental. Funders pick the assurance level per question as a price/quality dial, with per-tier time-to-quorum in the quote. Selfie-tier verdicts are labeled "live humans," never "unique humans."
8. **No on-chain challenge oracle.** Settlement runs directly from revealed votes. In its place: a commitment-based transparency log (aggregates + keyed per-rater commitments, recompute script, voucher-issuance vs verified-identity counts — never per-address plaintext), off-chain correlation and answer-fingerprint analytics feeding *future-round eligibility* only (never retroactive payout changes), verdict publication separated from payout finality (verdicts pend behind a tiered analytics pass with published per-tier SLOs; published verdicts carry tier-mix/diversity metadata), and a bounded remediation policy (defined trigger, capped, fee-refund-only, never paying a party implicated in manipulation).
9. **Fee: neutral caller-set `feeBps + feeRecipient`** on funding entry points (immutable cap 20%; default 0; no company address on-chain). RateLoop's interfaces charge 5–10% (start ~7.5%), quoted all-in on the x402 lane with a small fixed floor; funder-side only — raters are always free, with no paid rater conveniences (they would reopen the gambling analysis). The fee is held in escrow until settlement; **under-quorum rounds refund everything including the fee**.
10. **Single-operator keeper is a liveness convenience, not a trust point.** Everything it calls (reveal, settle, cleanup) is permissionless; a run-your-own-keeper script and optional Gelato/Chainlink backup are published. Payout claims are permissionless (`claim()` callable by anyone) — the auto-claimer is a convenience executor with self-claim as fallback.

## Mechanism (v0 default; iterate by redeploy on live data)

- Commit tuple: `{vote, prediction report, blinded payout commitment, nullifier, voucher}`. The prediction report survives only while scoring is RBTS-style — if a later iteration adopts correlated-agreement scoring, it is removed (it is the largest permanent per-task UX cost).
- Round lifecycle: free eligibility-gated commit → tlock reveal after the blind window (keeper-executed; **self-reveal fallback** if the drand beacon is late; beacon dead ⇒ full refund) → scoring inside `settleRound` from revealed data → payouts claimable ~1–2 minutes after the window. Funder cancellation only before the first commit (or with pro-rata rater compensation from the fee); no edits after open.
- Payouts: per-round, immediately legible — share of bounty weighted by *this round's* score through a **soft transform** ("base share + accuracy bonus"; an honest revealed vote never pays exactly zero; a hard zero-floor would punish honest minorities). **Cross-round statistics never enter the per-round price** — the production-validated pattern (Prolific/MTurk/Toloka pay flat-and-legible and enforce quality via gates): gold/known-answer items gate *eligibility attestations* (issuance, tier progression, revocation — never in-round settlement math, preserving the trust claim), cross-round stats live in the reputation/detection layer (round access, tiers, removal), and removal + verified identity is the deterrent (re-entry costs a new identity). Accepted residual: below-detection-threshold laziness earns until caught, taxed by tier caps.
- Probation: unverified wallets vote advisory-only until graduating on gold-question and minority-calibration evidence (rate-limited per identity cluster); **verified raters are payout-eligible immediately**. Advisory commits use the same shape minus voucher/nullifier/payout-commitment.
- Known adversarial edges, managed not solved: verified-identity majority capture becomes profitable around $250–800 bounties for small rounds at $30/account — bound max bounty per tier by round size, caps, and *detection capability*; real-human collusion rings cost nothing in forgery and are detectable only in answer-content statistics; the gold pool is a depleting security asset (rotation budget, delayed feedback for advisory accounts, per-item pass-rate drift monitoring); self-dealing funders can manufacture verdicts — hence verdict-pending analytics, diversity metadata, and funder CoI declarations in ToS.

## Contract schemas (recorded defaults)

- **Voucher:** `{voteKey, contentId, roundId, nullifier, tierId, expiresAt}`, EIP-712-signed by the issuer's backend signer. Engine checks signature, expiry, nullifier-unused, and the voteKey's signature over the commit payload. Nullifier = `H(identity_secret, contentId, roundId)` with a provider-stable per-identity base and a never-rotated derivation secret held apart from the signing key.
- **Issuer trust anchor:** the engine immutably references the issuer *contract*; the issuer keeps signer rotation as the one retained admin role (whitelisted in the assert-no-admin script), plus expiry, nonces, and issuance caps with an automatic rate circuit breaker.
- **Engine→escrow interface:** paginated `settleRound(contentId, roundId, cursor, count)` stores per-commit `weightBps`; escrows read `claimWeight()`; `heldFeeAmount` pushed at settlement, refunded on quorum-miss.
- **Claims:** `claim(commitKey, payoutAddress, salt)` against the blinded commitment, callable by anyone. Unclaimed shares revert to the funder after the stale grace. Salt custody: passkey-synced plus a server-held *encrypted* escrow copy recoverable via the identity layer (operator holds ciphertext only).
- **Content:** only hashes/commitments on-chain — never content payloads or plaintext URLs in structs/events; content is served from operator storage so takedown (de-list, de-pin, denylist) always works. Round kill = de-pin + de-list + funder-eligibility revocation + quorum-miss refund, with fee retention on ToS-violating content. Minimized event payloads throughout.

## UX invariants

**Rater:** one-time onboarding ≤ wallet + (at first payout) one combined get-paid sheet (verification + DAC7 + sanctions consent); per-task interaction ≤ answer + one prediction tap; no visible claim step (auto-executed, batched, sponsored); per-round earnings immediately legible as base share + bonus, anchored by a rolling 30-day accuracy stat; no leaderboards, streaks, or decay (crowdworker-ruling pattern) — habit comes from rater-set availability windows and the sealed→reveal notification loop. Failure states have designed copy: paymaster down → client-queued commit with deadline awareness (never "self-fund a key"); voucher denied → reason classes (re-verify / appeal / neutral legal); quorum miss → refund explanation + similar round; tax form → held balance always visible. Gold failures show an explanation, never touch already-earned payouts, and have a one-tap appeal with a human-review SLA and a published overturn rate. Platform failures are never billed to raters (keeper-fault reveal failures get a treasury make-good of the expected base share).

**Funder:** ≤3 decisions (question, audience/assurance, budget) via presets computed from the economics model ("Quick pulse — 15 live humans, ~30 min, $25"), with an advanced drawer for raw knobs; preview-as-rater doubles as the § 312j info block; question templates + AI linting + a cheap pilot-round preset with one-click scale-up; "filled or fully refunded" advertised at the pay button; the waiting room shows quorum progress → settle states → a watermarked provisional score during the analytics pass; result artifact = layered card with plain-language confidence, honest tier vocabulary, and the methodology link; a compliant self-verifying "Panel Verdict" share artifact (recomputes against the commitments). Primary B2B rail = prepaid workspace balance via a regulated partner (invoice/SEPA/card; VAT-ID at workspace level; B2B gate = self-declaration + VAT-ID, never document-KYB before a pilot round). Fees all-in pre-purchase, itemized on every receipt.

**Agent API/MCP:** quote → ask → wait → result; free unauthenticated quote endpoint; webhooks + polling + the MCP long-wait pattern; idempotency keys on `ask`; permanent sandbox (testnet USDC, deterministic simulated panels); versioned JSON-Schema results with a verdict-status enum (`pending_analytics | published | delisted | under_quorum_refunded`) and itemized fee fields; machine-readable per-tier SLOs (expected-time-to-quorum, expected-verdict-time); an API-key + prepaid-balance mode so agents can integrate without any wallet; x402 as the differentiated no-account lane, not the only door. Sales asset: a published benchmark of RateLoop panels vs GPT/Claude-as-judge (agreement with ground truth, LLM-judge failure cases, cost, latency) — positioned as the calibration/ground-truth tier of an eval stack.

## Phases

**Phase 0 — parallel backlog (runs alongside the build; nothing here blocks testing):**
1. Legal opinions: EMT/PSD2 on the funder-side escrow flow (+ EU interface-fee characterization); VAT deemed-supplier structuring memo + binding-ruling decision; confirmatory gambling-law check; the consolidated counsel list in the legal doc.
2. DAC7 build-out: BZSt DIP portal onboarding, the combined get-paid sheet (collected at payout-eligibility, never signup; advisory participation needs nothing), § 23 withhold mechanics, non-EU residency plausibility procedure.
3. Economics model: per-tier table (max bounty vs N_min vs caps vs detection lag; rater wage targets incl. unpaid-probation cost; quorum-failure rates; sponsorship/griefing budget incl. a fully-self-paid fallback line; gold-pool authoring/rotation budget) + a one-page revenue model — this table is also the funder-preset/quote/refund-prediction engine.
4. Demand work: use-case re-scoring, wedge selection (leading candidates: confidential creative/message pretesting; eval-panel-as-API), design partners; competitive positioning vs Mercor/Prolific/Surge (buyer-real differentiators: permissionless agent payment, provably sealed rounds).
5. Identity-and-role matrix: pin each legal duty to a layer — (i) on-chain one-time keys, (ii) stable pseudonymous rater ID at the receipt/DAC7 layer, (iii) vaulted real identity (retention-then-deletion).
6. Operational resilience: mapping-DB breach as the DPIA headline scenario (per-rater-key encryption; operator holds ciphertext); envelope-encrypted DAC7 vault; backup/DR; issuer key in KMS with issuance alarms + circuit breaker; founder continuity plan (second authorized person for the DAC7 filing, sanctions desk, takedown mailbox); drand/sequencer runbooks; pre-round content moderation + 18+ eligibility; regulated-partner selection for B2B balances; rater-surface funnel instrumentation ("open in browser" interstitials for in-app webviews).

**Phase 1 — specs:** (a) mechanism/round-lifecycle spec per the v0 defaults above (incl. the round state machine, quorum-refund and unclaimed-shares invariants, a gas benchmark for commit/settle); (b) agent API/MCP spec per the UX invariants (design-partner integration as acceptance test); (c) funder golden path.

**Phase 2 — contract surgery:** deletions first (LREP, governance, launch pool, oracle, frontend registry, stake machinery — decision-independent); then the new builds (engine settlement pipeline, escrow claim paths, voucher/nullifier commit checks, fee field, de-proxying per the wiring-cycle plan); prune LREP/governance/oracle/stake test suites; add invariants: no admin path to funds, engine holds no rater funds, funder exit always available, quorum-miss refunds the fee, beacon-dead refund.

**Phase 3 — services:** keeper reduced to reveal/settle/cleanup (+ self-reveal fallback support); transparency-log publisher; correlation + answer-fingerprint analytics and the verdict pipeline; sanctions screening at eligibility issuance + frontend; ponder cleanup; testnet enablement (keeper Base Sepolia support, testnet USDC/paymaster/facilitator); regenerate `@rateloop/contracts`.

**Phase 4 — app + SDK:** wallet-layer swap to the Base-native stack (concentrated in ~2 dozen thirdweb bridge/services/hooks files; ERC-7677 paymaster proxy carrying the ported eligibility/quota policy); remove governance/tokenomics/claim/launch/stake UX; eligibility-tier UX (earnings-menu presentation; counterfactual earnings during advisory play; entry-tier task bundling); checkout legal furniture; claim UX + salt recovery; failure-state copy matrix; earnings receipts; sealed→reveal notification loop; funder golden path + waiting room + result artifact; e2e rebuild for the new connector.

**Phase 5 — hardening at traction:** audit competition on the (small) immutable core, Immunefi bounty, soak, then the "final" keyless mainnet deployment; publish TRUST.md, the transparency log, the recompute script, and the keeper runbook.

## TRUST.md (published trust posture)

Immutable contracts (addresses + verification links); no admin keys; no operator path to funds — stated with its honest limits: funds are USDC and inherit Circle's freeze/blacklist power and depeg risk; sealing trust rests on the drand threshold network; vote admission and per-identity caps are operator-attested (published voucher-issuance vs verified-identity counts are the audit). Raters never deposit funds in the happy path; votes are sealed (tlock) and cast from single-use pseudonymous keys; one-vote-per-identity enforced by on-chain nullifiers; settlement recomputable against published commitments; bounded remediation policy; what we store, how it's encrypted, and what a breach would expose; bug policy = disclose → exit → redeploy; run-your-own-keeper instructions.
