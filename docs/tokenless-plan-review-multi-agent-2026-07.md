# Multi-Agent Review of the Tokenless Plan (July 2026)

**Status:** 2026-07-12. Five independent adversarial reviews (legal, mechanism/economics, technical-vs-repo, business, cross-doc consistency) of [web3-exit-stablecoin-pivot-analysis-2026-07.md](web3-exit-stablecoin-pivot-analysis-2026-07.md), [tokenless-immutable-implementation-plan-2026-07.md](tokenless-immutable-implementation-plan-2026-07.md), and [legal-revenue-assessment-tokenless-design-2026-07.md](legal-revenue-assessment-tokenless-design-2026-07.md). Findings triaged below; **Disposition** says what was done: *Fixed* (doc text corrected in this pass), *Amended* (design/plan changed in this pass), *Gate* (added to Phase 0 as a launch gate), *Open* (accepted, needs dedicated work, tracked here), *Rejected* (with reason).

## The five most important findings

1. **[Mechanism/Technical — CRITICAL] One-vote-per-identity was unenforceable under unlinkable one-time vote keys**, making the credential issuer a covert vote-stuffing key and inverting §A.6's core argument ("votes are the one input the operator cannot forge"). **Amended:** commit vouchers now carry a deterministic per-(identity, round) nullifier (`H(identity_secret, contentId, roundId)`), deduped on-chain at commit; the transparency log publishes per-round voucher-issuance vs verified-identity counts so issuance volume is auditable. Residual honesty cost stated in §A.6: vote *admission* is Hawig-attested — the trust claim is "operator cannot forge or tamper with *submitted* votes and cannot steer settlement math," not "operator cannot mint voters."
2. **[Mechanism/Technical — CRITICAL] The "merkle root" payout-routing option reintroduced an operator-posted root over escrowed USDC** — the exact pattern §A.6 rejects. **Amended:** struck. Raters bind a blinded payout commitment (hash of payout address + salt, or stealth meta-address) into their own on-chain commit; settlement and claims are self-contained from user-submitted inputs; Hawig posts nothing. Residual linkability (claim-time reveal, amount-correlation in small rounds, funding-graph linkage for self-funded keys) is now stated rather than implied away; unlinkability requires sponsored or fresh-funded keys.
3. **[Mechanism — CRITICAL] The incentive core was asserted, not designed.** Removing the penalty side, paying share-of-bounty (constant-sum normalization), and hard zero-floor truncation each independently void RBTS's strict truthfulness guarantee; with sponsored free entry, lazy consensus-voting is EV-positive (~0.8·B/N risk-free vs an informed premium of ~0.05–0.2·B/N). The literature (Shnayder et al. multi-task peer prediction; Gao et al.; "Truth Serum for Sharing Rewards") exists precisely because of these failure modes. **Amended:** Phase 0 item 3 upgraded from "mechanism review" to **mechanism redesign** with a concrete direction: multi-task/correlated-agreement scoring across a rater's rounds, operator gold questions gating payout eligibility, soft score transforms instead of a hard floor (a hard floor also punishes honest minority reports — the thing RBTS exists to reward), reputation-gated compounding income so detected laziness is ruin. Also: the Phase 1 commit tuple now explicitly includes the *prediction report* (without it, "RBTS" degrades to herding-rewarding output agreement) and the binary-signal/n≥3 preconditions.
4. **[Legal — HIGH] EU fake-review law was entirely missed.** The product commissions paid ratings; UWG Anhang Nr. 23b/23c (via the Omnibus Directive) blacklists presenting commissioned reviews as consumer feedback, and § 5b Abs. 3 UWG imposes disclosure duties on anyone making ratings accessible — structurally unmeetable if scores are framed as "reviews" (raters are paid strangers and may be AI agents). **Gate + Fixed:** product framing is *paid panel research / pretesting*, never "reviews"; funder ToS must prohibit presenting scores as organic consumer feedback and require "paid panel" disclosure in any public use; § 5b-grade methodology page; added to the counsel list.
5. **[Business — CRITICAL] No demand validation anywhere in a five-phase plan.** The docs' own research says answer quality + the agent API gate adoption, x402 organic demand is small, and the old use-case scoring assumed a World-ID/on-chain moat — yet no wedge use case is named, no design partner exists, and the agent API has no spec. **Gate:** Phase 0 now includes demand validation (re-score use cases under tokenless assumptions; pick a wedge — the strongest candidates surfaced: confidential creative/message pretesting for brands, and eval-panel-as-API for agent builders; 3–5 design-partner LOIs before Phase 2 contract surgery) and a one-page volume/rater-wage model. Competitive reality check recorded: Mercor ($10B valuation, ~$85/hr experts), Prolific (~$350M annualized, "100% Human Guarantee" — proof-of-human as a feature without crypto), Surge/Scale, Human API, HumanLayer. Buyer-real differentiators are narrow and should lead the pitch: permissionless agent payment (no sales call) and provably sealed rounds; escrow/immutability differentiates for BaFin, not buyers.

## Consistency review

All 18 findings accepted; the mechanical fixes (stale governance/multisig language, "USDC micro-bonds," frontend-bond conversions, on-chain World ID references, oracle hedging, 1–5% vs 5–10% fee drift, "hard-coded fee" remnants in §A.1/Part B/Phase 2, plaintext transparency-log remnants, missing Phase 0 gates, keeper-screening delta, checkout furniture in Phase 4, stale header) were applied to the three documents in this pass. The plan header now reflects all seven decision rounds.

## Net assessment

The architecture and legal work held up well under adversarial review — no reviewer found a reason to reverse the tokenless/keyless/stake-free/on-chain-voting decisions, and the two CRITICAL design holes (nullifier, payout commitment) had clean fixes that *strengthen* the design. The genuinely weak flanks are (1) the incentive core, which needs a real mechanism-design pass (multi-task scoring + gold questions) before contract surgery, and (2) the business layer — demand validation, security-budget/wage economics, and the paid-panel-not-reviews framing — which is now gated in Phase 0 rather than assumed. The unified identity-and-role matrix (one-time keys / stable receipt pseudonym / vaulted identity, with each legal duty pinned to a layer) is the single deliverable that resolves five separate legal findings at once.
